What was this page made for?

I made this website in an attempt to simplify task management for students like myself. Outlook is helpful, but I find it easier to put all of my tasks and exams in one place so I know where to look when planning my days, weeks, and perhaps months.

The idea is pretty simple. Write down what you need to do and when its due, tag how urgent it feels, and give yourself a rough time estimate so you can plan accordingly.

Why so minimalistic?

Typically students use outlook to schedule out classes, exams, meetings, tasks, etc. I find that simplifying the process of tracking tasks into simple to-do this such as list is much more helpful in getting ahead, allowing time to relax, or even preparing for going out of town.

Best ways to use it?

Depending on how you like to plan, you can use this site on a certain day of the week to prepare for the week or even month ahead. Some students may put tasks into the tracker to get everything done at once in order to cram before a deadline, while others may use it to prepare for the future and avoid stress. Either way, it's up to you!

Why does it look so plain?

The design is kept intentionally simple to avoid attracting too much attention away from the tasks themselves. Putting the tasks in a simple format allows for quick decision-making on what to do next without getting distracted. The color palette is kept minimal as well to not overwhelm students, especially during stressful times or midnight study sessions.
